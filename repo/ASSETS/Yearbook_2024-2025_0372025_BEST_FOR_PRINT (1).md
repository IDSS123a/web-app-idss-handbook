INTERNATIONALE DEUTSCHE SCHULE SARA JEVO

Throughout the year, our students have shown us what learning can truly become
when it is met with curiosity, resilience, and sincerity. Whether through academic
competitions, creative projects, or the quiet victories of everyday school life, they’ve
reminded us that success often lives in the details, not in grand gestures. Our
teachers, for their part, have continued to grow—not out of obligation, but from a
deep sense of professional integrity that I profoundly respect. Their efforts don’t
seek applause; they seek impact.
We are also approaching a quiet yet important moment in our school’s story: our
tenth anniversary. Ten years may not seem long in the life of an institution, but for us,
they represent the forging of identity, trust, and direction. This school did not simply
grow—it was shaped, challenged, questioned, and redefined, again and again. That, to
me, is a sign of something alive.
This yearbook is both a record of everything we’ve lived through together and a
glossy collection of images that remind us where we’ve been. Behind every image lie
conversations, doubts, laughter, corrections, and growth. That is what makes a school
more than a building: the continuous presence of real human effort.
To the parents who look beyond test scores, to the students who ask better questions
than we sometimes have answers for, and to the colleagues who understand that
education is a living, evolving practice—I thank you.
The future calls for more than slogans; it demands continuity and heartfelt care.
The story of this school is still being written.
Warm regards,
Principal

GRADE 4
In fourth grade, we begin to find our own way as learners.  We take more
responsibility for our work and our time, and it feels good t o be trusted.
Planning, organizing, and reflecting become part of ho w we learn. We’re not
just following instructions — we’re setting goals and thin king about how to
reach them. When we get stuck, we try different strategies and learn to  ask
the right questions. We read to understand, not just to fini sh, and we write
with purpose and voice. Math becomes more about thinkin g and less about
memorizing. Being independent doesn’t mean doing everything  alone. It
means knowing when to ask for help, when to help someon e else, and when
to trust ourselves. Every day, we grow more confident in o ur thinking and
more aware of the kind of learners — and people — we’re b ecoming.

GRADE 5
In fifth grade, everything starts to feel a little more real. We notice the shift
— in expectations, in our thinking, and in how we carry  ourselves. There’s
more to manage, more to balance, but also more space to grow. We begin to
connect what we learn with how the world works and who we  are in it.
Subjects become deeper, and questions become bigger. We l earn to speak
up for our ideas and to listen when others do the same. Mistak es are part of
the process, and so is learning how to reflect and try agai n. With each new
challenge, we build confidence — not all at once, but  slowly and steadily.
Crossing into middle school isn’t a sudden leap, but a thoug htful walk
forward. We are no longer just students of lessons, but expl orers of our own
voice, choices, and identity. It’s a journey we take to gether, with support
from teachers who believe in us and classmates who grow al ongside us.
