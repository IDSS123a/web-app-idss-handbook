**Instructions for Taking Minutes**

Minutes serve as a document that confirms the work and determines the form and flow of the meeting!

**The minutes must contain:**

1. **The title of the meeting** *(with the meeting number), e.g., Minutes from the 1st Parent Meeting in the 2024/2025 school year*
2. **Location** *the place of the meeting*
3. **Date** *date of the meeting*
4. **Time** *the start time of the meeting (in hours)*
5. **The number and names of those present** (names and surnames of parents and staff)
6. **The number and names of those absent** (names and surnames of the absent, and whether *the absence is justified or not)*
7. **A list of attendees** *(those who can participate in the work but do not have the right to vote)*
8. **Establishing a quorum** *(confirmation that the necessary number of members for valid decision-making is present)*
9. **The name of the chairperson** *(not necessary if prescribed by some school act)*
10. **The name of the secretary** *at the meeting*
11. **The agenda** *– the meeting is held according to the agenda items*
12. *The chairperson* ***PROPOSES THE AGENDA*** *and submits it for approval to those present who accept it by voting –* ***THEY CAN ALSO AMEND IT***
13. The record of the meeting:

- **the progress of the meeting** and the subjects discussed, including the names of those who participated and a brief summary of their statements
- **voting results** on individual proposals or agenda items
- **a dissenting opinion** of any member, if requested to be entered into the minutes

1. **The end** or interruption time of the meeting
2. **A note of any attachments** that are part of the minutes (if any)
3. **Signatures of the chairperson and the secretary**

**During the meeting:**

- *The chairperson explains the topic/proposal*
- *A discussion on the topic is opened*
- *A decision is made – by voting (* ***it can be public or secret*** *)*
- *For making a decision, the chairperson asks* ***who is FOR, who is AGAINST, and who is ABSTAINED***
- *The method of secret voting is prescribed by the school act*
- *The secretary records everything under the name and surname of the speaker in the first person*
- *By summarizing*

**The course of the meeting can be recorded:**

If anyone leaves or joins the meeting later, the secretary must note this in the minutes (record the name and surname and the time of departure/arrival).
