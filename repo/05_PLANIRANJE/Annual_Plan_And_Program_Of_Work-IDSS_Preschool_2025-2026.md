 S t r a n i c a  1 Purpose of the Document  
 
The primary purpose of this document is to thoroughly define, structure, and present the 
annual plan and work program of the German preschool group for the academic year 
2025/2026. The document serves not only as a practical framework for day -to-day 
operations but also as a long -term strategic guid eline aimed at creating and maintaining 
a stimulating, safe, and developmentally appropriate educational environment for 
preschool children. Its intention is to ensure that the group operates in alignment with the 
highest contemporary pedagogical, psycholo gical, and educational standards 
recognized both locally and internationally.  
The document encompasses all crucial components of educational work, including the 
systematic planning of objectives and activities, the careful implementation of teaching 
and learning practices, and the comprehensive evaluation of outcomes to guarantee 
effectiveness and continuous improvement. It outlines a structured approach to early 
childhood education that prioritizes holistic child development and emphasizes the 
importance of individualized learning paths. Particular attention is given to activities t hat 
encourage curiosity, creativity, problem -solving, and critical thinking from an early age, 
ensuring that children develop the foundational competencies required for lifelong 
learning.  
In preparing this program, special consideration has been devoted to the guidelines, 
frameworks, and recommendations contained in essential reference documents such as 
the Curriculum for Early Childhood Education in Baden -Württemberg , the Baden -
Württemberg Framework Plan for Early Childhood Education , and the Handbuch für die 
frühkindliche Bildung und Erziehung in Baden -Württemberg . These authoritative resources 
are invaluable because they provide evidence -based principles for creating 
educational content t hat fosters balanced and sustainable growth across all domains of 
a child’s personality. They highlight the interconnection between cognitive, emotional, 
social, and physical development, ensuring that the educational process contributes 
equally to intelle ctual readiness, emotional resilience, interpersonal cooperation, and 
physical well -being.  
The program has therefore been carefully designed to secure a comprehensive and 
child -centered approach to learning. This includes the organization of activities around 
meaningful themes, the application of innovative and research -based methodologies, 
the thoughtful integration of modern didactic and technological resources, and the 
establishment of continuous feedback mechanisms for assessing progress. Flexibility and 
adaptability are central principles: the program is intended to evolve in response to the  
developmental pace, interests, and individual needs of each child, thereby fostering 
both academic growth and personal self -confidence.

 S t r a n i c a  2 Educational Context  
 
The German preschool group functions within the broader framework of P.P.U. 
International Montessori House Sarajevo , yet it is distinguished by a pedagogical vision 
that expands upon and complements the traditional Montessori philosophy. While 
Montessori principles of independence, freedom within structure, and experiential 
learning remain integral, the program incorpo rates additional elements specifically 
tailored to prepare children for the transition into elementary school. This dual 
orientation —respecti ng the individuality of each child while systematically introducing 
foundational school skills —marks the program as unique in its approach.  
A central focus of the curriculum is to cultivate the child’s readiness for formal schooling 
by gradually developing essential academic skills such as early literacy, pre -writing, and 
numeracy, as well as the ability to understand and apply spatial and tem poral concepts. 
At the same time, equal emphasis is placed on cultivating non -academic dimensions of 
development, such as concentration, persistence, self -regulation, empathy, and the 
ability to collaborate effectively with peers. Activities are deliberate ly designed to nurture 
autonomy, responsibility, and self -confidence, all of which are indispensable qualities for 
a smooth and successful transition to elementary school.  
An additional distinguishing feature of the program is its close physical and pedagogical 
connection to P.U. Internationale Deutsche Schule Sarajevo . By being housed in the 
premises of the elementary school, the preschool group enjoys regular and natural 
contact with primary school students, teachers, and the overall school community. This 
proximity allows preschoolers to become familiar with the rhyt hm, routines, and 
expectations of a school setting in a gradual and non -intimidating manner. They are 
provid ed with opportunities to participate in selected school events, observe classroom 
environments, and interact socially across age groups, thereby gaining a sense of 
belonging to a larger educational community.  
Such integration with the elementary school environment significantly reduces the 
anxiety often associated with the transition from preschool to formal education. Through 
daily interactions, children are not only introduced to the academic expectations of 
school but also learn to navigate its social and emotional dimensions. They experience 
first-hand the values of respect, cooperation, and intercultural understanding that 
characterize the school, which further supports their holistic development. In this w ay, the 
German preschool group fulfills its dual mission: to respect and nurture the uniqueness of 
every child while equipping them with the knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary to 
thrive in elementary school and beyond.

 S t r a n i c a  3 The Role and Importance of Planning  
 
Planning represents one of the most essential pillars in securing a high -quality and holistic 
educational experience within the German preschool group. A well -structured plan does 
not merely outline daily activities, but rather provides a comprehensive framework that 
guides the entire educational process throughout the year. Careful planning allows 
educators to define precise developmental and educational goals, ensuring that the 
learning experiences offered to children are purposeful, meaningful, and 
developmentally appropriate. By organizing thematic units, selecting age -appropriate 
pedagogical methods, and aligning activities with the specific interests, talents, and 
learning styles of children, pla nning becomes a powerful tool for fostering both academic 
readiness and social -emotional growth.  
Beyond setting goals, strategic planning encompasses ongoing monitoring, systematic 
evaluation, and reflective practice. These processes allow teachers to track children’s 
progress over time, identify areas where additional support is needed, and introduce  
timely modifications to the program. In doing so, the preschool remains flexible, 
responsive, and aligned with each child’s unique developmental trajectory. The annual 
plan and program also function as a guiding document for the entire preschool team, 
ensuring consistency, transparency, and coherence in the delivery of educational 
activities. Teachers, assistants, and support staff rely on this shared framework to 
coordinate their work effectively, avoiding fragmentation and ensuring that every child 
benef its from a seamless educational journey.  
Planning also extends to building strong and meaningful communication with parents. 
Parents are regarded as crucial partners in the educational process, and the exchange 
of information between home and preschool ensures that children receive consistent 
guidance, encouragement, and reinforcement in all environments. Through regular 
parent -teacher meetings, progress reports, and open dialogue, planning strengthens the 
bond of trust between families and the institution, creating a supportive triangle of child –
parent –teacher. This collaboration not only enriches the child’s learning experience but 
also supports their continuous development beyond the formal setting of the preschool.  
Ultimately, effective and forward -thinking planning contributes to establishing a nurturing, 
stimulating, and well -structured environment where children feel safe, motivated, and 
inspired to learn. It lays the foundation for exploration, curiosity, and ind ependence, while 
simultaneously preparing children for a confident and successful transition into primary 
education.

Staff and Their Roles  
 
The strength and success of the German preschool group lies in the commitment and 
professionalism of its staff, who work collaboratively to create a safe, stimulating, and 
caring environment for children. The team consists of pedagogues, teachers, and 
admi nistrative personnel, each with clearly defined responsibilities that together form a 
coherent system of support for both children and families.  
Pedagogues play a central role in designing, coordinating, and implementing the 
educational program. Their responsibility includes aligning daily activities with the 
principles of modern preschool education, while also tailoring content to the 
developmenta l needs of the group and individual children. They engage in continuous 
observation and assessment, ensuring that each child is progressing in cognitive, 
emotional, social, and physical domains. Furthermore, pedagogues take a leading role 
in fostering stro ng partnerships with parents, offering guidance, feedback, and support 
to ensure that the child’s learning and development extend seamlessly from the 
preschool into the home environment.  
Teachers provide essential support in the daily execution of activities. Their responsibilities 
extend beyond instruction to include active participation in play, organization of both 
structured and unstructured activities, and fostering a positive classro om climate. 
Teachers help children navigate interpersonal relationships, encourage cooperation, 
and provide the scaffolding needed for children to build new skills and confidence. They 
are also responsible for ensuring that children’s safety and well -being  remain the top 
priority in every aspect of preschool life.  
The administrative staff, while less visible in daily classroom interactions, play a vital 
supportive role. Their duties include logistical organization, resource management, event 
coordination, and effective communication with parents and the wider school

Work Organization  
 
The academic year 2025/2026 officially begins on Monday, September 1, 2025 , and 
concludes on Friday, August 28, 2026 . The preschool operates five days a week, Monday 
through Friday, from 08:00 to 17:00 , ensuring that children receive consistent daily routines 
that support both structured learning and free exploration.  
• Arrival time:  between 07:30 and 09:00  
• Pick-up time:  no later than 17:00  
• Non-working days:  Saturdays, Sundays, and official school/public holidays  
This schedule has been carefully designed to support both children’s developmental 
needs and the organizational requirements of families. The daily rhythm balances 
educational activities, creative play, outdoor experiences, meals, rest, and individual or 
small-group work. Through this routine, children acquire a sense of structure and 
predictability, which contributes to their emotional security and positive social behavior.

children for future academic and social challenges.

Methodology  
 
The methodological approach  of the IDSS preschool group is grounded in the principles 
of active learning  and child -centered education. Children are not passive recipients of 
knowledge but active participants who construct their own understanding through 
exploration, experimentation, and interaction.  
Teachers employ a variety of pedagogical strategies, including direct instruction, guided 
discovery, project -based learning, play -based activities, and group cooperation . These 
methods are complemented by the use of didactic resources that stimulate creativity 
and inquiry. Activities are always designed to meet the individual learning needs  of 
children, allowing them to progress at their own pace while being challenged to reach 
higher levels of competence.  
Particular emphasis is placed on hands -on learning , where children explore materials, 
test hypotheses, and express their ideas creatively. Games, experiments, role play, and 
storytelling are used as tools to encourage imagination and critical thinking. By

 S t r a n i c a  11 Evaluation and Assessment  
 
The evaluation of children’s progress  is continuous, systematic, and multifaceted. 
Assessment is not limited to academic skills but also includes social, emotional, physical, 
and creative development. Teachers use observations, portfolios, children’s work 
samples, developmental checklists, and individual interviews  to build a holistic picture of 
each child’s growth.  
Parents are actively involved in this process through regular meetings, progress reports, 
and open communication channels . The aim of evaluation is not to compare children 
with one another, but to recognize their unique abilities, learning styles, and personal 
achievements . By identifying strengths and areas for improvement, educators can adapt 
the program to better suit the needs of each child and ensure meaningful progress.  
 
 
 
Materials and Resources  
 
The preschool uses a rich variety of materials and resources  carefully selected to meet 
the developmental stages of the children. All materials are aligned with pedagogical 
principles that support learning through play, creativity, and exploration.  
• Didactic Materials : Puzzles, building blocks, Montessori materials, math and literacy 
games, science tools, and language learning resources.  
• Creative Materials : Crayons, paints, fabrics, clay, natural and recycled items for 
art and crafts.  
• Sports Equipment : Balls, ropes, hoops, obstacle -course equipment, and props for 
movement and balance activities.  
• Spatial Resources : Stimulating classrooms with thematic corners (reading, art, 
science, drama) and outdoor areas such as gardens and playgrounds, which 
allow for daily outdoor activity.  
These resources provide children with opportunities for manipulation, experimentation, 
and creative self -expression , while also fostering independence, fine motor skills, and 
environmental awareness.  
 
 
 
Educational Goals  
 
The program is designed to support children’s comprehensive development  and prepare 
them for the transition to primary school. Key goals include:  
1. Language Development  – Building competence in German and English, with 
additional support in B/H/S when needed.  
2. School Readiness  – Ensuring children are emotionally, socially, and academically 
prepared for first grade.  
3. Pre-literacy and Numeracy Skills  – Introducing children to letters, sounds, and 
numbers through play -based activities.
